# Module 2: Digital Twin Technology

Welcome to Module 2 of the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics course. In this module, we'll explore the critical concept of digital twins in robotics, focusing on how virtual representations of physical robots enable safer development, testing, and optimization of robotic systems.

## Overview

Digital twin technology has revolutionized robotics development by providing virtual environments where robots can be designed, tested, and refined before deployment in the real world. This module covers:

1. **Introduction to Digital Twins**: Understanding the fundamental concepts of digital twin technology in robotics
2. **Gazebo Simulation**: Hands-on experience with the Gazebo physics simulator for robotics
3. **Unity Robotics**: Exploring high-fidelity rendering and simulation using Unity
4. **Sensor Simulation**: Techniques for simulating various robot sensors in virtual environments

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:
- Understand the concept and importance of digital twins in robotics
- Set up and configure Gazebo simulation environments
- Create realistic physics simulations for humanoid robots
- Implement sensor simulation for LiDAR, cameras, and IMUs
- Develop plugins for extending simulation capabilities
- Integrate Unity for high-fidelity rendering

## Prerequisites

Before starting this module, ensure you have:
- Completed Module 1 (ROS 2 fundamentals)
- Basic understanding of physics concepts
- Familiarity with 3D modeling concepts
- Access to a computer with sufficient processing power for simulation
